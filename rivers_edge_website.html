Local Knowledge

Did You Know?

Quad Cities Tree & Lawn Facts

01

Emerald Ash Borer has devastated millions of ash trees across Iowa and Illinois — making early detection and treatment critical for Davenport and Quad Cities homeowners.

02

A single mature tree can absorb up to 48 pounds of CO₂ per year and provide enough oxygen for two people — making proper tree care an investment in your environment.

03

Healthy, well-maintained trees can increase property value by 10–20%, according to the USDA Forest Service — one of the best returns any homeowner can make.

04

Iowa's growing season spans roughly 150–170 frost-free days, making spring and fall the most critical windows for lawn treatment and tree health care in the Quad Cities.

05

Mississippi River Valley proximity creates unique soil moisture and frost patterns throughout Davenport and the Quad Cities — conditions that directly impact how your lawn and trees respond to treatment.

06

Dutch Elm Disease wiped out millions of elm trees across the Midwest and remains an active threat today. Routine health assessments are the best line of defense.

07

Native Iowa clay soils tend to be heavy and compacted, which can cause nutrient lock-out and root stress — making professional core aeration and soil health analysis essential for a thriving lawn.

08

Neglected trees decline fast. A healthy oak or maple can live 100–300+ years, but without proper pruning, pest management, and structural support, decline can begin in just a few seasons.
